The Digital Divide

The Digital Divide refers to the gap between those who have access to computing resources and those who do not. It affects education, economic opportunity, and communication across the world. People in rural or low-income areas often face limited internet access, creating long-term disadvantages. Addressing the divide means improving digital equity and creating solutions that reach underserved communities.
